Why This Platform Exists

A quiet, sourced response to the noise around Islam online.

Our Mission

Clarity before argument

Islam is discussed constantly online — often by people who have never opened a classical text, and just as often defended in ways that don't actually engage the question being asked.

Unmasking Anomalies exists to slow that down. We write and record material that explains complex data and concepts on their own terms first, and only then addresses the criticism or misconception in question — with sources a reader can check for themselves.

Our Approach

Three commitments

Sourced, not shouted. Every claim traces back to a text or a citation the reader can verify.

Context first. Verses, hadith, and historical events are read in the setting they occurred in, not lifted out of it.

No ridicule. We respond to ideas, not to the people holding them — including when we disagree strongly.

What Guides Us

Our Values

Textual honesty

We quote sources in full context and link to them, so nothing rests on our word alone.

Good faith engagement

We steelman the strongest version of an objection before responding to it.

Patience over speed

We'd rather publish something accurate next month than something rushed today.

Open to correction

If a claim we've made turns out to be wrong, we update it publicly — not quietly.

Since 2021

How We Got Here

2021

A small group, a shared frustration

Founded by a handful of writers tired of seeing the same misreadings repeated online with no sourced response.

2022

First long-form article series

Published a series walking through the preservation of the Qur'an, chapter by chapter of the objection.

2024

Video became the main format

Recorded discussion and reaction videos, reaching a wider audience than written articles alone.

2026

Where we are now

A growing library of articles and videos, organized by topic, still built one sourced answer at a time.
